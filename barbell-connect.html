Project Overview

Weightlifting can be an isolating sport. Barbell Connect is a platform built to change that by connecting lifters with gyms, coaches, and each other. The goal was to make weightlifting feel like something you do together, whether you're just starting out or competing seriously.

Project Goals

  • Make it easy to find gyms that actually fit what you're looking for
  • Lower the barrier for beginners so they feel welcome, not intimidated
  • Make gym discovery feel exciting rather than overwhelming
  • Show that weightlifting isn't just for a certain type of person
  • Build a real support network between lifters, gyms, and coaches

Impact

A few specific problems this solves:

Lower barrier to entry

Connecting newcomers with gyms and resources that are actually beginner-friendly.

Gym discovery that works

Filter by what matters to you: equipment, vibe, distance, community size.

A broader view of the sport

Showing weightlifting as something for everyone, not just a specific body type or background.

Real connections

Bringing lifters, gyms, and coaches into the same space so they can actually support each other.

Who is this for?

Everyone!

Anyone in the weightlifting world, really:

  • Beginners looking to start their weightlifting journey
  • Amateurs seeking to improve their skills and find training partners
  • Experts wanting to connect with like-minded lifters
  • Coaches looking to expand their reach and support more athletes

Key Features

  • Gym Discovery: Search and filter gyms based on location, equipment, and community vibe
  • User Profiles: Show who you are and what you're training for
  • Community Feed: Share progress, connect with other lifters, and stay motivated
  • Coach Directory: Find and connect with certified coaches and trainers
  • Event Listings: Discover local competitions, workshops, and community events

Design Approach

The design needed to feel energetic without being overwhelming. A few of the key decisions:

  • Yellow and black: high energy, easy to read, instantly recognizable
  • Simple navigation that doesn't ask new users to figure anything out
  • Clear hierarchy so the important stuff always stands out
  • Mobile-first, because that's where lifters actually use it
  • Imagery that reflects the full range of people who lift

Reflection

Barbell Connect pushed me to think hard about serving two very different users at once. Beginners need to feel welcomed and not overwhelmed; experienced lifters need enough depth to make it worth their time. Getting that balance right meant making a lot of intentional tradeoffs. It was a good reminder that inclusive design isn't just about aesthetics. It's about who gets to feel like the product was built for them.

Key Takeaway: Weightlifting is better when it's not done alone. Barbell Connect makes that possible.
