Project Overview

Arrived is a privacy-first location sharing app designed to help families and friends confirm safe arrivals without compromising personal privacy. This team project applied Goal Directed Design methodology to create a focused, trust-centered experience.

Working collaboratively, we conducted user research, developed detailed personas, and designed core screens that balance safety needs with privacy concerns.

  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Team Project)
  • Duration: 12 weeks
  • Team: 3 designers
  • Tools: Figma, Goal Directed Design
  • Deliverables: Personas, wireframes, high-fidelity screens, design system

Project Goals

  • Apply Goal Directed Design methodology to solve real user problems
  • Create personas based on research to guide design decisions
  • Design a minimal location sharing experience that prioritizes privacy
  • Develop a calm, trustworthy visual system
  • Validate design decisions through persona-driven scenarios

Research & Personas

Following the Goal Directed Design approach, we developed two personas to represent our target users and their distinct needs.

Mark - Primary Persona

Mark, a 50-year-old accountant from Springfield, Massachusetts, values family above all else. He seeks simplified, user-friendly technology that helps him stay connected without overwhelming complexity. His primary goals include:

  • End Goals: Simplified interfaces, clear privacy controls, reminders and scheduling features
  • Life Goals: Own his accounting firm, travel the world
  • Experience Goals: Seamless work-life balance, intuitive tools that support time management

Mark's need for privacy and simplicity directly informed our core design decisions.

Emily - Secondary Persona

Emily, a 21-year-old college student and aspiring wedding planner from Auburn, Alabama, represents our secondary user group. Her goals focus on staying connected with friends and family while building her career:

  • End Goals: Establish herself professionally, build industry connections, feel fulfilled
  • Life Goals: Thrive as a wedding planner, maintain relationships, travel for inspiration
  • Experience Goals: Feel confident and supported, make positive impact

Emily's social connectivity needs balanced Mark's privacy requirements, helping us design for both cautious and socially active users.

Wireframes & Validation

Using our personas as guides, we mapped out key user paths and validation points. We focused on three essential flows:

  • Key Path (Pink): Mark's primary journey - selecting trusted contacts and sharing location with minimal friction
  • Validation Paths (Blue): Critical decision points where users control privacy settings, manage contacts, and configure notifications

The wireframe validation board helped us identify where Mark would need reassurance about privacy and where Emily would want quick social sharing options.

Design Process

Based on Mark's need for simplicity and privacy, we designed three core screens:

  • Splash Screen - Establishes trust immediately with clear 'I've Arrived' messaging and calming visual identity. Addresses Mark's need for straightforward, confidence-building interfaces.
  • Profiles Screen - Enables users to manage trusted contacts with intuitive selection. Designed for Mark's preference for clear controls without overwhelming options.
  • Map View - Shows approximate location rather than precise coordinates, directly addressing Mark's privacy concerns while meeting Emily's need to stay connected.

Each screen was designed with our primary persona's goals in mind: only share what's necessary, when it's necessary.

Visual Design & Branding

We designed a visual system that reinforces trust and calm—critical for Mark's comfort with technology and Emily's social use cases:

  • Color system: Blues and greens evoke safety and trust, addressing Mark's need for reassurance
  • Typography: Clear hierarchy optimized for quick scanning, supporting both Mark's preference for simplicity and Emily's on-the-go usage
  • Components: Reusable UI elements with accessible contrast for critical safety information
  • Tone: Calm, non-intrusive design that respects privacy while enabling connection

Final Design

The final screens reflect our persona-driven design decisions:

  • Splash Screen - Welcoming and trust-building, designed for Mark's need for clear, reassuring interfaces
  • Profiles Screen - Simple contact management that gives Mark control without complexity
  • Map View - Privacy-first location sharing using approximate positioning, directly addressing Mark's reluctance to share precise personal information

Each decision traced back to persona goals—Mark's need for privacy and simplicity, Emily's need for quick social connection.

Component System

We developed a component library that serves both personas' needs:

  • Button styles optimized for touch and clarity
  • Input fields with clear validation states
  • Status indicators for location sharing and privacy settings
  • Contact cards designed for quick recognition
  • Privacy controls that put Mark in control without overwhelming him

Impact & Learnings

This project demonstrated the power of Goal Directed Design. By grounding every decision in Mark and Emily's goals, we avoided feature bloat and maintained focus on solving real user problems.

Key Outcomes

  • Created a focused MVP that addresses primary persona needs without overwhelming complexity
  • Developed a privacy-first approach that could differentiate in a crowded market
  • Validated design decisions against persona goals rather than assumptions
  • Built a scalable design system for future development

What I Learned

Working through the Goal Directed Design methodology taught me to:

  • Let persona goals drive design decisions rather than personal preferences
  • Balance competing user needs (Mark's privacy vs Emily's social connectivity)
  • Validate each design choice against specific user goals
  • Recognize when simplicity serves users better than features

Next Steps

If continuing this project, we would:

  • Conduct user testing with people matching our persona profiles
  • Design onboarding that establishes trust for privacy-cautious users like Mark
  • Build notification system that respects user preferences
  • Test privacy controls with actual users to validate our assumptions

Key Takeaway: Goal Directed Design creates better products. By deeply understanding Mark's reluctance to share location and his need for simple, trustworthy tools, we designed an experience he would actually use—not just one that looked good.
